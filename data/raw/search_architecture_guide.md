Vector databases represent text chunks as dense floating-point arrays generated by embedding models. Cosine similarity is calculated as the dot product of two normalized vectors, reflecting their semantic closeness. In lexical search, BM25 assigns query relevance scores using term frequency and inverse document frequency. Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF) combines dense and sparse ranks by summing reciprocal ranks, bypassing raw score scaling issues. Rerankers employ cross-encoder architectures to analyze token attention relationships directly between query and context blocks.
