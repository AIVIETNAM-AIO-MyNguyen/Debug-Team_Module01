Cross-encoder rerankers process query-document pairs jointly through a transformer to produce fine-grained relevance scores. Unlike bi-encoders, cross-encoders attend to both query and document tokens simultaneously enabling richer interaction modeling. Reranking is typically applied as a second-stage process after an initial fast retrieval pass to re-order the candidate set. Latency increases linearly with the number of candidates reranked since each pair requires a full forward pass.
